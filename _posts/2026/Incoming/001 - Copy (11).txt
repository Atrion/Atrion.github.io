Not everyone on the road looks hostile. Some are pleasant, friendly, and confident—yet quietly lost in the most dangerous way.

<!--more-->

### Ignorance

The pilgrims meet **Ignorance**, a man who believes he can reach heaven by sincerity and good deeds. He is not aggressive or crude. He simply assumes that being “good enough” will be enough, and he rejects the necessity of Christ and His righteousness.

This is one of the most sobering portraits in Bunyan: a man who is polite, certain, and wrong. Ignorance is not saved by good intentions. He needs a Saviour, not self-confidence. A false hope can carry a man far—right up to the gates—without ever bringing him in.

**Proverbs 16:25 (KJV)**  
> There is a way that seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death.

Sincerity without Christ cannot save.
