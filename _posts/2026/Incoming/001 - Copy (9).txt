Some pits are not dug in the ground—they’re dug in the mind, where darkness settles and hope feels far away.

<!--more-->

### Doubting Castle

Captured by **Giant Despair**, the pilgrims are thrown into **Doubting Castle** and suffer in heaviness. They are beaten down and nearly convinced that escape is impossible. The darkness becomes a voice, urging them to give up.

But then comes one of the most precious moments in the journey: they remember the **key of Promise**. What they needed was not new strength, but remembered truth. God’s promises open the doors that despair tries to lock. The way out was not earned—it was granted.

**Psalm 42:11 (KJV)**  
> Why art thou cast down, O my soul? and why art thou disquieted within me? hope thou in God: for I shall yet praise him, who is the health of my countenance, and my God.

God’s promises are the key to escaping despair.
